## Ingredients

- 1 large shallot, cut into large chunks
- 6 garlic cloves
- 1 habanero, roughly chopped (seeds optional)
- 1 bunch flat-leaf parsley, leaves only
- 1 (6-inch) piece fresh lemongrass, trimmed, pounded, and thinly sliced
- 1 1/2 inches fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
- 3 Tbsp canola oil, divided
- Kosher salt (Diamond Crystal)
- 3 large heirloom tomatoes (about 1 lb), coarsely chopped
- 8 oz green beans, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 1/2 lbs wild Atlantic salmon, skin removed, cut into 1-inch chunks
- Fish sauce (optional), to taste
- White rice, for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. Add the shallot, garlic, habanero, half the parsley leaves, lemongrass, and ginger to a food processor. Pulse, scraping down the bowl as needed, until it forms a coarse paste. About 1 to 2 minutes.
2. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium-high. When the oil shimmers, add the paste and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant and starting to caramelize, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the tomatoes and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until wilted and softened, about 5 minutes. Add the green beans and 1/4 teaspoon salt and cook until bright green, about 3 minutes. Add 2 to 3 tablespoons of water if you want it saucier.
4. Season the salmon with salt and pepper. Push the skillet mixture to the side and add the remaining 1 tablespoon oil. Place the salmon on the oil, spoon the tomato mixture over the top, and cook undisturbed for 2 minutes. Gently stir and cook 2 minutes more. Turn off the heat and let it rest 1 minute. The salmon should flake easily and the green beans should be crisp-tender. Taste and season with fish sauce if using.
5. Serve over white rice or on its own
